    Introduction to embodied carbon, LCA and sustainable construction¶

    From OneClickLCA¶

    Link: https://academy.oneclicklca.com/courses/introduction-to-embodied-carbon-lca-and-sustainable-construction

    Part 1 - What is LCA¶

    Glosary¶

    LCA - Life Cycle Assessment EPD - Environmental Product Declaration

    Types of Assessments¶

    Cradle to Gate Assessment - Assessment from stage A1 - A3. Cradle to Practical Completion Assessment - Assessment of complete stage A. UpfrontCarbon - Carbon emission in Cradle to Practical Assessment. Cradle to Grave Assessment - Assessment for complete stages A, B and C. Cradle to Cradle Assessment - Everything in Cradle to Grave assessment, and Stage D.

    Impact Indicators¶

    1. Carbon Footprint - Assessment that only talks about GWP.

    Notes¶

    LCA¶

    • LCA is a standardized methodology to calculate the impact of buildings on the environment throughout it's entire lifecycle.
    • Life cycle of a building starts at raw material extraction, and ends at Deconstruction, Reuse, or recycling.

    Stages based on European Standard 15978¶

    A1 - A3 Product(ion) Stage (Manufacturing Impacts)¶

    • A1 - Raw material extraction (like mining limestone, or extracting clay for bricks, etc.) (Does it also include all cleaning and purification stages?)
    • A2 - Transport from mines to manufacturing site. (manufacturing site here could be cement factory, brick kiln, steel, etc.)
    • A3 - Manufacturing (the actual manufacturing process, baking, forming, casting, etc.)

    A4 - A5 Construction Stage (Building Manufacturing Stage)¶

    • A4 - Transport to construction site
    • A5 - Installation / Assembly (Impacts also include Energy use, water use, and construction waste)

    B1 - B7 Use Stage (Material Related Impacts)¶

    • B1 - Use
    • B2 - Maintenance
    • B3 - Repair
    • B4 - Replacement
    • B5 - Refurbishment
    • B6 - Operational Energy Use
    • B7 - Operational Water Use

      (Energy, and water used for the next 40, 50, or 100 years - based on the building life) Not sure if these strictly fall inside the Use Stage, but they are classified as B6 and B7

    C1 - C4 End of Life Stage¶

    • C1 - Deconstruction & Demolition
    • C2 - Transport (from demolition site to recycling facility)
    • C3 - Waste Processing
    • C4 - Disposal (includes transport to landfill, and impacts after landfill, some decompose and release methane, some do not decompose, and pollute soil. These impacts are captured here)

    D1 - Benefits and loads beyond system boundary¶

    • Reuse, recovery and/or recycling potentials expressed as net impacts and benefits.

    Maybe some material from A1 - A3 are being reused, so we do not need to mine / extract again. Maybe some waste is reclaimed (steel can be reused) from C3. When we incinerate Timber, or other organic material, we recover the energy. (how?)

    Note¶

    • Most regulations currently focus on the B6 stage - Operational Energy Use (Energy Efficiency)

    A1-A3 #CradleToGate assessment with very narrow scope. Looks only into production of materials.¶

    A4-A5 #CradleToPracticalComplemetion assessment. If we assess only the carbon emissions, we can refer to this as #UpfrontCarbon¶

    CradleToGrave assessment is a whole life-cycle assessment.¶

    CradleToCradle assessment is when Stage D is included in CradleToGrave assessment. Basically paving way for the next generation of material use by reuse / recycling from the previous generation.¶

    EmbodiedCarbon - If we exclude B6 and B7 from Cradle to Cradle (Operational Energy and Water use), and focus only on carbon emissions. All carbon emissions here are somehow related to materials. Either on manufacturing, transport, installation, maintenance, repairs, and end-of-life treatment.¶

    There are many variations of Embodied Carbon Assessments. Some exclude A5. Depends on whether these parts contribute significantly or not. Depends on where these parts can be measured accurately.

    LCA Outputs¶

    • Outputs are indicators and NOT the final impact.
    • Example, indicator could be the increase in Carbondioxide emissions, that could cause global warming. But does not measure the actual global warming, or the polar melt.
    • That's why it's Global Warming Potential (GWP) and not Global Warming Impact (we don't know if it has impacted?)

    Environmental Indicators¶

    Also known as Environmental Impact Categories

    1. Global Warming Potential (GWP) (KgCO2e)¶

    GWP describes how much a product contributes to climate change. LCA that concerns only this impact category is called Carbon Footprint.

    2. Acidification (SO2e)¶

    Acidification describes how much the product decreases the pH level, and may cause acidification of soil, water, and cause acid rain.

    3. Eutrophication (PO4^3 e)¶

    Eutrophication describes the flow of nutrients to ecosystems, resulting in (excess) algae growth. Can be measured in Phosphorus (CML / Recipe) or Nitrogen (TRACI) equivalent.

    4. Ozone Depletion (CFC-11-eq)¶

    Ozone Depletion describes the damage caused to the Ozone layer in the stratosphere.

    5. Tropospheric Ozone (c4H4e)¶

    Tropospheric Ozone describes the quantity of summer smog causing gases emitted. Can also be measured as NitrousOxide equivalent (NOxeq)

    6. Depletion of fossil resources (MJ)¶

    Depletion of fossil resources describes how much fossil resources are withdrawn. Can also be measured in Kg oil equivalent

    It is hard to measure, and pinpoint some numeric value to these. So, characterization is done.

    1. Define a unit for each impact.
      1. for GWP it is KgCO2e (Kilos of CO2 equivalent) since CO2 is contributing the most in global warming - also remember it is just the potential.
    2. How other measurement units (Methane, CFC Emissions, etc.) can be translated to KgCO2e. A characterization factor / translation factor is used to convert these units
    3. Methane will have a factor of 30. Because it is a more potent Greenhouse gas. So, 1 unit of CH4 will be around 30 units of KgCO2e
    4. All other emissions have their factors.

    Characterization Methods¶

    • Europe uses Characterization Factor (CML) and North America uses TRACI method.
    • ReCiPe is not widely used in the construction industry.

    These have different target units / standard units, and emission factors.

    Part 2 - Why is it important to perform LCA¶

    • 28% from operational emissions
    • 11% from materials and constructions (as embodied carbon)

    EPD - Environmental Product Declaration¶

    EPD is LCA for a product with additional rules for calculation, verification, and publication. * EPD should / is based on real data. No forecasting / predicting. So, it is always recorded and analyzed. (If a factory wants to release EPD for a new product, the energy rating / impacts / LCA needs to be done for 1-2 years, and then EPD calculated based on data) * EPD is valid for 5 years - need to be renewed. * EPD can represent one product from one factory (more accurate) and many products from many factories (a composite material for example) * EPDs offer knowledge on product's environmental performance.

    Only similar products that are calculated with same methods can be compared. * Scopes * Cradle-to-gate * Cradle-to-grave * Cradle-to-gate with options
