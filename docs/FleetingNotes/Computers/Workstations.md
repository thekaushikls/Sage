# Workstations
Created : 24-06-2022 08:32

* The purpose of a workstation, is to enable what is defined as work.
* Work is can be Writing , Authoring, Research and Documentation, Presentation making, Accounting, Programming, etc.
* Work can also be in the fields of media, gaming, game development, graphics and multimedia, or even a mix of all these.
* Which could mean, any computer that helps get a job done could be termed as a workstation? What if the work is gaming? Maybe in that case, it is better to switch to a dedicated gaming laptop.
* Workstations seem to focus mainly on Processor performance, Ram availability, and Hard Disk speed, i.e. has all ingredients to make the computer multi-task, and perform tasks as fast as possible.
* A great example of 'A workstation that is not a gaming laptop' can be the Macbook Pro. I have been using it for a couple of years, and I can definitely say it is one of the ~~best~~ convinent laptops out there.
* It has good spec-to-performance ratio, but is not (at all) suitable for gaming inspite of having a solid graphics card.
