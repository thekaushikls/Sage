# Embodied Carbon
Created : 11-08-2022 12:55

* Embodied Carbon is the calculation of the amount of carbon emission from a building before occupancy.
* It includes emissions from 
	* Mining / Extraction / collection of raw materials.
	* Cleaning, Purification, and quality testing.
	* Manufacturing, quality testing, etc.
	* Transportation, Fabrication, wastage
* The goal of this measurement is to reduce the amount of carbon emitted (carbon footprint) of built structures.
* According to EPD(Environmental Protection Declaration ISO 14025) material manufacturers provide the GWP(Global Warming Potential) values via prior LCA(Life-Cycle Assessment) of their products. This is multiplied with the quantity of the material used, to quantify the footprint.
* One of the ways to create carbon-negative structures is to use plants (store carbon from the atmosphere) as the primary building material. Eg: Hemp-crete.
