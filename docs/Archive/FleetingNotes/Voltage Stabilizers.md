## What is a Voltage Stabilizer?

A voltage stabilizer is an electrical device / circuit that helps in maintaining a steady voltage supply.
This ensures that the device connected to a stabilizer is not damaged.

> Why does a voltage fluctuation damage an electrical appliance? Why does Current / Ampere fluctuation not damage?

## How does it work?

A stabilizer works on the principle of transformer. Current is transferred from the primary winding (coil) to the secondary winding. Depending on any fluctuation, the windings of the secondary coil is altered using relays.

> Since V = IR (roughly), increasing the resistance (by increasing the length of wire / coil) compensates for low voltage. Similarly, decreasing the wire length compensates for high voltage.
> 
> This means, current (Amperes) can vary safely without damaging the appliance.

These movable parts are called relays. Relays move based on the voltage input, to stabilize the output.

> Example:
> R1 operates between 170V and 200V
> R2 operates between 200V and 230V
> R3 operates between 230V and 260V
> 
> when relays come into operation, it usually makes a 'tak-tak' sound - which is usually audible, and noticeable.
