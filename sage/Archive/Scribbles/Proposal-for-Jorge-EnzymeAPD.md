I understand that you want to create an in-house Computational Design team at your company. The team will primarily focus on creating, and implementing digital strategies to solve project-related inefficiencies across the company.

I'd like to borrow the analogy from [Tackle the monkey first](https://blog.x.company/tackle-the-monkey-first-90fd6223e04d); 

*The Monkey:*
Spending time on identifying issues and inefficiencies, and building a MVP(s) to validate solutions would be the most impactful path. It helps one to validate ideas quickly, without investing too much time. If solutions do not work, or as in most cases - users find it hard to adopt the proposed solutions, failing fast is possible if the scope of the task is not humongous.

*The Pedestal:*
Building a dedicated team might be easier with a lot of computational designers ready for work in the market. But, without a direction, and a strategy to implement, and a strategy to measure success, it quickly becomes resource(time and money) intensive.

For these reasons, I propose to tackle this requirement in two phases.

PHASE 1: Building lots of MVPs / The Monkey Phase

Under this phase, the main aim is collect as much information as possible about inefficiencies, pain points, and try out small scale solutions, and collect feedback from users. This is to make sure we do not end up creating tools / solutions, that nobody wants to use / that the users find really hard to adopt. Naturally, all ideas, solutions, will be documented, so it can be used in PHASE 2. Further, results from Phase 1, will also direct us in understand what type of people / skills to hire for the future in-house team.

*Full-disclosure:* I have worked on many monkeys, and many more pedestals over the last few years.

Scope of Work
1. Understand the current workflow of the company, identify inefficiencies / bottlenecks.
2. Discuss with other employees involved in projects, to understand pain points in their workflows.
3. Conduct research, and propose solution(s) along with feasibility in terms of time, and resources.
4. Pitch and present feasible solutions to team, validate idea(s), and collect feedback.
5. Build / deploy solution(s) as MVP(s), and promote user adoption across the company.
6. Provide technical support, train employee(s) to use solutions / tools developed.
7. Create a feedback loop, make it easier for users to approach the CD team.
